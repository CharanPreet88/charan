  • Fitness refers to the state of being physically fit and healthy, involving various components such as:
    1. Cardiovascular endurance:
      The ability of the heart and lungs to supply oxygen to the muscles during physical activity.
    2. Muscular strength:
      The capacity of muscles to exert force against resistance.
    3. Muscular endurance:
      The ability of muscles to perform repetitive movements over an extended period.
    4. Flexibility:
      The range of motion in joints and muscles.
    5. Body composition:
      The proportion of fat, muscle, and other tissues in the body.

  • Wellness encompasses a broader approach to overall health, including physical, mental, emotional, and sometimes even spiritual well-being. It involves various dimensions:
    1. Physical wellness:
      Maintaining a healthy body through proper nutrition, exercise, adequate sleep, and avoiding harmful habits.
    2. Mental wellness:
      Having a positive mindset, managing stress effectively, fostering healthy relationships, and engaging in activities that promote mental health.
    3. Emotional wellness:
      Understanding and managing emotions, having resilience, and seeking support when needed.
    4. Social wellness:
      Building and maintaining healthy relationships, fostering a sense of belonging, and contributing to the community.
    5. Spiritual wellness:
      Finding purpose and meaning in life, exploring personal beliefs, and nurturing a sense of inner peace.
